Exercise 1 (Martingales Warm-up).

Do enough of these exercises to feel comfortable with them.

  1. 1.

    Assume that Xn is an integrable (ℱn) adapted stochastic process which is decreasing, i.e.

    Xn+1≤Xn a.s..

    Prove that Xn is a supermartingale.

    Remark.

    Similarly an adapted increasing process is a submartingale.

    Solution.

    We simply use the monotonicity of the conditional expectation to obtain the last property of supermartingales

    𝔼⁢[Xn+1|ℱn] ≤𝔼⁢[Xn|ℱn]=Xn.∎
  2. 2.

    Let (Xn)n∈ℕ, (Yn)n∈ℕ be ℱn (sub-)martingales, prove that

    1. (a)

      (Xn+Yn)n∈ℕ is a (sub-)martingale.

      Solution.

      We have 𝔼⁢[|Xn+Yn|]≤𝔼⁢[|Xn|]+𝔼⁢[|Yn|]<∞ and ℱn-adapted follows from the fact that sums of measurable functions are measurable. What is left to show is

      𝔼⁢[Xn+1+Yn+1|ℱn]=𝔼⁢[Xn+1|ℱn]+𝔼⁢[Yn+1|ℱn]=Xn+Yn

      or the same with ≥ for submartingales respectively. ∎

    2. (b)

      (Xn∨Yn)n∈ℕ is a submartingale,

      Solution.

      Adapted and integrable is easy to check, and the submartingale property follows from the monotonicity of conditional expectation

      𝔼⁢[Xn+1∨Yn+1|ℱn] ≥{𝔼⁢[Xn+1|ℱn]≥XnXn≥Yn𝔼⁢[Yn+1|ℱn]≥YnYn≥Xn
      =Xn∨Yn.∎
  3. 3.

    Let (Xn)n∈ℕ0 be a (ℱn)-submartingale. Let T be (ℱn)-stopping time.

    1. (a)

      Suppose that (Xn)n∈ℕ0 is bounded and T is a.s. finite (the meaning is different from ”T is a.s. bounded”!). Prove that 𝔼⁢[X0]≤𝔼⁢[XT]<∞.

      Solution.

      Since T<∞ a.s. we have

      XT=limn→∞⁡XT∧n=limn→∞⁡XnT

      where XT=(XT∧n)n∈ℕ is a bounded submartingale, because

      • •

        as ℱn∧T⊆ℱn as n∧T≤n therefore XT is adapted

      • •

        we have integrability due to

        𝔼⁢[|XnT|] =𝔼⁢[∑k=1n|Xk|⁢𝟙T=k]+𝔼⁢[∑k=n+1∞|Xn|⁢𝟙T=k]
        ≤𝔼⁢[∑k=1n|Xk|]+𝔼⁢[|Xn|⁢𝟙T>n]
        ≤∑k=1n𝔼⁢[|Xk|]+𝔼⁢[|Xn|]<∞
      • •

        Lastly we have the submartingale property

        𝔼⁢[Xn+1T-XnT|ℱn] =𝔼⁢[(Xn+1T-XnT)⁢(𝟙T≤n+𝟙T>n)|ℱn]
        =𝔼⁢[(Xn+1T-XnT)⁢𝟙T>n|ℱn]
        =𝟙T>n⁢𝔼⁢[Xn+1T-XnT|ℱn]⏟≥0

      As Xn is bounded, XT is bounded and therefore 𝔼⁢[XT]<∞. By dominated convergence we further have

      𝔼⁢[XT] =𝔼⁢[limn→∞⁡XnT]=limn→∞⁡𝔼⁢[XnT]≥𝔼⁢[X0T]=𝔼⁢[X0]∎
    2. (b)

      Suppose that there exists a constant K>0 such that for ℙ-a.e. ω∈Ω,

      |Xn⁢(ω)-Xn-1⁢(ω)|≤K,∀n∈ℕ.

      We also suppose that 𝔼⁢[T]<∞. Show that 𝔼⁢[X0]≤𝔼⁢[XT]<∞.

      Solution.

      XT is a submartingale for the same reason as before, and we can again use dominated convergence if we find an integrable upper bound. This upper bound is T⁢K+|X0| because

      XnT =X0+∑k=0T∧n-1Xk+1T-XkT
      ≤|X0|+∑k=0T-1K=|X0|+T⁢K.∎
    Hint.

    Compare this with statements about martingales in the lectures and use the bounded stopping time T∧M for some constant M∈N and the dominated convergence theorem.
