Exercise 1 (Lemma 6.3.5).

Let (Xn) be an (ℱn)-submartingale and S,T be bounded stopping times such that S≤T a.s. Prove that, 𝔼⁢[XS]≤𝔼⁢[XT].

Hint.

Consider the stochastic integral of H=(Hn) against X with Hn:=1S≤n-1<T.

Solution.

Since S,T are stopping times, we have that

Hn:=𝟙S≤n-1<T=𝟙S≤n-1⁢𝟙T>n-1=𝟙S≤n-1⁢(1-𝟙T≤n-1)

is ℱn-1 measurable and therefore that the process H=(Hn) is previsible. The stochastic integral

(H⋅X)n =∑k=1n𝟙S≤k-1<T⏟=𝟙S+1≤k⁢𝟙k≤T⁢(Xk-Xk-1)=∑k=(S+1)∧nT∧n(Xk-Xk-1)
=XT∧n-XS∧n.

is a submartingale since Hn is positive. Since S,T are bounded, there exists N∈ℕ such that

𝔼⁢[XT]-𝔼⁢[XS] =𝔼⁢[XT∧N-XS∧N]=𝔼⁢[(H⋅X)N]≥𝔼⁢[(H⋅X)0]=0.∎
