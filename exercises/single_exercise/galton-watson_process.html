Exercise 1 (Galton-Watson Process with finite second moment).

Let (Xt,t∈ℕ0) be a branching process, starting from X0=m∈ℕ0. Let (ξi(t),i∈ℕ,t∈ℕ0) be the iid, ℕ0-valued offspring resulting in the following population development

Xt+1=∑i=1Xtξi(t),n≥1.

Note that we simply ignore the children ξi(t) for parents i>Xt who do not exist. We denote by μ the average number of childs one parent has 𝔼⁢[ξ1(0)]. We define a filtration ℱ with

ℱt:=σ(ξi(k):k<t,i∈ℕ).

Assume that the offspring variance is finite, σ2:=Var⁢(ξ1(0))<∞, to prove that, for μ>1, our martingale M=(Mt)=(μ-t⁢Xt) is an L2-martingale. Calculate 𝔼⁢[M∞] and Var⁢(M∞).

Solution.

By Blackwell-Girshick we have

Var⁢(Mt+1) =μ-2⁢(t+1)⁢Var⁢(∑i=1Xtξi(t))=μ-2⁢(t+1)⁢(𝔼⁢[Xt]⏟=μt⁢𝔼⁢[Mt]⁢Var⁢(ξ1(t))⏟=σ2+Var⁢(Xt)⁢𝔼⁢[ξ1(t)]2⏟=μ2)
=μ-(t+2)⁢σ2⁢𝔼⁢[M0]⏟=m+Var⁢(Xt)⁢μ-2⁢t⏟=Var⁢(Mt)

Using Var⁢(M0)=0, we get

Var⁢(Mt) =∑k=0t-1[Var⁢(Mk+1)-Var⁢(Mk)]
=σ2⁢m⁢∑k=0t-1μ-(k+2)=σ2⁢m⁢μ-2⁢1-μ-t1-μ-1=σ2⁢m⁢1-μ-tμ⁢(μ-1)
≤σ2⁢mμ⁢(μ-1)

We therefore have

supt∈ℕ0⁡𝔼⁢[|Mt|2] =supt∈ℕ0⁡Var⁢(Mt)+𝔼⁢[Mt]2≤σ2⁢mμ⁢(μ-1)+m2<∞,

which implies that M is an L2 martingale. In particular we have Mt→M∞ in L2 and therefore also in L1. By Sheffé’s Lemma we therefore have

𝔼⁢[M∞]=𝔼⁢[|M∞|]=limt→∞⁡𝔼⁢[Mt]=m

and by the L2 convergence lemma we have

Var⁢(M∞) =𝔼⁢[M∞2]-𝔼⁢[M∞]2
=(limt→∞⁡𝔼⁢[Mt2]⏟=Var⁢(Mt)+m2)-m2
=σ2⁢mμ⁢(μ-1).∎
