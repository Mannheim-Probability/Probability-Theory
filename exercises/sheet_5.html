Exercise 1 (Uniform Integrability).
  1. 1.

    (Prop. 6.3.14 (ii)⇒(i)) (Xα,α∈I) a family of random variables with supα∈I⁡𝔼⁢[|Xα|]<∞, i.e. bounded in ℒ1⁢(Ω,ℱ,ℙ). Suppose that for every ϵ>0, there exists δ>0, such that for all A∈ℱ with ℙ⁢(A)<δ, we have

    𝔼⁢[|Xα|⁢𝟙A]<ϵ,∀α∈I.

    Prove that the Xα are uniformly integrable.

    Solution.

    Let ϵ>0. By the Markov inequality we have

    ℙ(|Xβ|≥M)≤𝔼⁢[|Xβ|]M≤supα∈I⁡𝔼⁢[|Xα|]M≔cM.

    So for all M≥M0:=⌈cδ⁢(ϵ)⌉ and for all β∈I, we have

    ℙ(|Xβ|≥M)≤δ(ϵ),

    where δ⁢(ϵ) is selected such, that by assumption we get

    𝔼⁢[|Xα|⋅𝟙|Xβ|≥M]≤ϵ ∀α∈I.

    Since δ⁢(ϵ) does not depend on β, it holds for all β∈I. So in particular we have

    supα∈I⁡𝔼⁢[|Xα|⋅𝟙|Xα|≥M]≤ϵ ∀M≥M0.

    As ϵ>0 was arbitrary, this is by definition uniform integrability. ∎

  2. 2.

    Prove that a sequence of identically distributed random variables {Xi,i∈ℕ} with 𝔼⁢[|X1|]<∞ is uniformly integrable.

    Solution.

    Since 𝔼⁢[|X1|]<∞, we deduce by the DCT

    limM→∞⁡𝔼⁢[|X1|⁢𝟙|X1|>M]=𝔼⁢[|X1|⁢limM→∞⁡𝟙|X1|>M⏟=0]=0.

    Therefore for every ϵ>0, there exists M>0, such that

    𝔼⁢[|X1|⁢𝟙|X1|>M]<ϵ.

    The sequence is identically distributed, therefore, for every i∈ℕ, we conclude that

    supi∈ℕ⁡𝔼⁢[|Xi|⁢𝟙|Xi|>M]=𝔼⁢[|X1|⁢𝟙|X1|>M]<ϵ.∎
  3. 3.

    Let (Xi,i∈ℕ) be a sequence of i.i.d. random variables with 𝔼⁢[|X1|]<∞. Let Sn=∑i=1nXi for every n∈ℕ. Then the family {1n⁢Sn,n∈ℕ} is uniformly integrable.

    Solution.

    Using 2, (Xi,i∈ℕ) is uniformly integrable. By Proposition 6.3.14 (Theorem 6.24 in Klenke), for every ϵ>0, there exists δ>0 (dependent on ϵ but not on i), such that for every measurable set A with ℙ⁢(A)<δ,

    supi∈ℕ⁡𝔼⁢[|Xi|⁢𝟏A]<ϵ.

    Then for every n∈ℕ,

    𝔼⁢[|Sn|n⁢𝟙A]≤1n⁢∑i=1n𝔼⁢[|Xi|⁢𝟏A]<ϵ.

    We also have (Sn/n,n∈ℕ) is bounded in L1:

    supn∈ℕ⁡𝔼⁢[|Sn|n]≤supn∈ℕ⁡1n⁢𝔼⁢[∑i=1n|Xi|]=𝔼⁢[|X1|]<∞.

    Then by Proposition 6.3.14 (Theorem 6.24 in Klenke), (Sn/n,n∈ℕ) is uniformly integrable. ∎

Exercise 2 (Not Uniformly Integrable).

Let (Un,n≥1) be i.i.d. Bernoulli random variables with ℙ(Un=1)=p∈(0,1) and ℙ(Un=0)=1-p. Set τ:=inf⁡{n≥1:Un=1}. Define Xn:=(1-p)-n⁢𝟙τ>n.

  1. 1.

    Show that (Xn) is a martingale (choose a suitable filtration).

    Solution.

    Choose ℱn=σ⁢(U1,…,Un). Because of

    {τ>n}={U1=0,…,Un=0}∈ℱn,

    Xn is adapted, and we have

    𝔼⁢[Xn|ℱn-1] =(1-p)-n⁢𝔼⁢[𝟙τ>n⁢𝟙τ>n-1|ℱn-1]
    =(1-p)-n⁢𝔼⁢[𝟙τ>n-1⁢𝟙Un=0|ℱn-1]
    =(1-p)-n⋅𝟙τ>n-1⋅ℙ(Un=0)
    =(1-p)-n⋅𝟙τ>n-1
    =Xn-1.

    By induction and positivity of Xn we also have 𝔼⁢[|Xn|]=𝔼⁢[Xn]=𝔼⁢[X0]=1<∞. So Xn is a martingale. ∎

  2. 2.

    Prove that Xn→0 almost surely

    Solution.

    As Xn is a martingale, we have

    1=𝔼⁢[Xn]=(1-p)-n⁢𝔼⁢[𝟙τ>n],

    so we get

    ∑n∈ℕ0ℙ(𝟙τ>n>ϵ)=∑n∈ℕ0ℙ(τ>n)=∑n∈ℕ0(1-p)n<∞.

    By Borell-Cantelli we therefore have

    ℙ(𝟙τ>n→0) =1-ℙ(∃ϵ>0,∀n0∈ℕ∃n≥n0:𝟙τ>n>ϵ)
    =1-ℙ(⋃ϵ>0⋂n0∈ℕ⋃n≥n0:{𝟙τ>n>ϵ})
    =1-limϵ→0⁡ℙ(lim supn{𝟙τ>n>ϵ})⏟=0 (BC).

    𝟙τ>n→0 almost surely implies there exists N⁢(ω) large enough such that 𝟙τ>n=0 for all n>N⁢(ω). So we can conclude that

    Xn⁢(ω)=(1-p)-n⋅𝟙τ>n⁢(ω)=0

    for all n>N⁢(ω). Thus, Xn→0 a.s. ∎

  3. 3.

    Prove that (Xn,n≥1) is not uniformly integrable.

    Solution.

    If (Xn)n∈ℕ was u.i., then Xn→X∞ in ℒ1 and 𝔼⁢[X∞]=1 by Theorem 6.3.16. This is however a contradiction to X∞=0 a.s. ∎

Exercise 3 (Tail σ-Algebra).
  1. 1.

    𝒜 is trivial if an only if all (𝒜,ℬ⁢(ℝd))-measurable functions are constant

    Solution.

    Assume that 𝒜 is trivial and let f:Ω→ℝd be (𝒜,ℬ⁢(ℝd))-measurable, then there exists y∈f⁢(A) and therefore

    ∅≠f-1⁢({y})∈𝒜={∅,Ω}

    therefore f-1⁢(y)=Ω which implies f⁢(ω)=y for all ω∈Ω.

    Assume on the other hand that, that 𝒜 is not trivial, then there exists some set A∈𝒜 with ∅≠A≠Ω. Then 𝟙A is measurable but not constant. ∎

Let X1,X2,… be a sequence of random variables with τn=σ⁢(Xn,…) and tail σ-algebra τ=⋂nτn.

  1. 1.

    Prove that lim infn⁡Xn and lim supn⁡Xn are τ measurable.

    Solution.

    It is sufficient to prove measurability on a generator. In particular it is enough if the preimages of the sets (-∞,λ] for all λ∈ℝ are measurable. Now for every N∈ℕ we have

    {lim infnXn≤λ} ={limn→∞infk≥nXk≤λ}=⋂n=1∞⋃k≥n{Xk≤λ}
    =⋂n=N∞⋃k≥n{Xk≤λ}⏟∈τn⁣⊆τN∈τN

    therefore the set is in τ. Replacing inf with sup and unions with intersections and vice versa proves the same result for the lim sup. ∎

Exercise 4 (Gambler’s ruin).

Let (Xn)n≥1 be a sequence of i.i.d. random variables with ℙ(X1=1)=1-ℙ(X1=-1)=p for some p∈(0,1), p≠1/2. Let a,b∈ℕ with 0<a<b. Define S0≔a and for every n≥1, Sn≔Sn-1+Xn. Finally, define the following stopping time:

T≔inf⁡{n≥0:Sn=0⁢ or ⁢Sn=b}.

We consider the filtration generated by (Xn)n≥1.

  1. 1.

    Show that 𝔼⁢[T]<∞.

    Solution.

    We have for every n≥0, ℙ(T≤n+b|ℱn)>inf(pb,(1-p)b)>0. We deduce from Sheet 5, Exercise 5 that 𝔼⁢[T]<∞. ∎

  2. 2.

    Consider for every n≥0,

    Mn≔(1-pp)Sn and Nn≔Sn-n⁢(2⁢p-1).

    Prove that (Mn)n≥0 and (Nn)n≥0 are martingales.

    Solution.

    (Sn)n≥0 is adapted to the filtration (ℱn)n≥0 generated by (Xn)n≥0 and then so are (Mn)n≥0 and (Nn)n≥0. They are integrable: clear for (Nn)n≥0 and comes from |Sn|≤a+n for every n≥0 for (Mn)n≥0. Then for every n≥0,

    𝔼⁢[Mn+1|ℱn]=Mn⁢𝔼⁢[(1-pp)Xn+1]=Mn⁢[p⁢1-pp+(1-p)⁢p1-p]=Mn,

    and

    𝔼⁢[Nn+1-Nn|ℱn] =𝔼⁢[Xn+1]-(2⁢p+1)=[p-(1-p)]-(2⁢p-1)=0.∎
  3. 3.

    Deduce the values of ℙ(ST=0) and ℙ(ST=b).

    Solution.

    We apply optional stopping Theorem to obtain that (Mn∧T)n≥0 is a martingale. Since T<∞ a.s., we have limn→∞⁡Mn∧T=MT a.s. We also notice that this process is bounded:

    |Mn∧T|≤max⁡((1-pp)b,1),n∈ℕ.

    By dominated convergence,

    𝔼⁢[MT]=limn→∞⁡𝔼⁢[Mn∧T]=𝔼⁢[M0].

    That is,

    (1-pp)a=𝔼[(1-pp)S0]=𝔼[(1-pp)ST]=ℙ(ST=0)+(1-pp)bℙ(ST=b). (1)

    Since ℙ(ST=b)=1-ℙ(ST=0), we get

    ℙ(ST=b) =(1-pp)a-1(1-pp)b-1,and ℙ(ST=0)=(1-pp)b-(1-pp)a(1-pp)b-1.∎
  4. 4.

    Compute the value of 𝔼⁢[T].

    Solution.

    Recall that the process (Sn∧T)n≥0 is bounded by b. For every n≥0, we then get

    |Sn∧T-(2⁢p-1)⁢n∧T|≤b+(2⁢p-1)⁢T∈L1.

    Then from dominated convergence theorem

    a=𝔼⁢[N0]=𝔼⁢[Nn∧T]=𝔼⁢[Sn∧T-(2⁢p-1)⁢n∧T]→𝔼⁢[ST-(2⁢p-1)⁢T].

    Finally

    𝔼⁢[T]=𝔼⁢[ST]-a2⁢p-1 =b2⁢p-1⁢(1-pp)a-1(1-pp)b-1-a2⁢p-1.∎
