Exercise 1 (Brownian Motion).

Recall that (Xt,t≥0) called a (centred) Gaussian process, if for every sequence of finite times 0≤t1<⋯<tk, k∈ℕ, (Xt1,…,Xtk) is (centred) Gaussian vector.

  1. 1.

    Suppose that (Xt,t≥0) is a continuous centred Gaussian process with Cov⁢(Xs,Xt)=s∧t for all s,t≥0. Show that (Xt,t≥0) is a standard Brownian motion.

  2. 2.

    A stochastic process (Xt)0≤t≤1 is called a Brownian bridge if: it is a continuous centred Gaussian process with covariance function given by

    Cov⁢(Xs,Xt)=s⁢(1-t),s≤t.

    Let (Bt,t≥0) be a standard Brownian motion. Show that the process

    Xt=Bt-t⁢B1,t∈[0,1]

    is a Brownian bridge. Moreover, prove that B1 is independent of (Xt,t∈[0,1]).

    Hint.

    please check the definition of independence; read e.g. Definition 2.14, Remark 2.15 and Theorem 2.16 in Klenke.
